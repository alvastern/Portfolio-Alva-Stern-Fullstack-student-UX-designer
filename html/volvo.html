Lock app: Volvo Cars

Collaboration with Volvo Cars, spring 2023

Description of the Project

In this project, me and my project group collaborated with Volvo Cars, where the task was to design and prototype a digital interface of an application. The goal was to design a digital car key based on research and requirements.

Goal of the project

The goal of this project was to create a user-friendly application that allows users to lock and unlock a car, share a car, view sharing history and set conditions for the sharing based on their personal preferences.

Design problems

There is a demand for an app-solution that allows digital car sharing with family and friends.

Older and less connected cars does not always posses the expected equipment and technology.

Background

Car keys are turning more digital, there’s therefore a growing demand to enable this to older cars.

With a solution developed by Volvo, less connected cars can be retrofitted to receive the same technology.

Design process

1. Preparatory work: Domain knowledge, Desktop Research

Domain knowledge

I acquired domain knowledge to ensure I had sufficient understanding to deliver an appropriate solution for the application and identify the target group.

Desktop research

I conducted desktop research by reviewed existing applications for digital car keys to get an fundamental understanding of specifik functions and the target group.

2. Research: Interviews, Compile research material

Interviews

Semi-structured interviews was conducted with people with and without driver license. My goal with the interviews was to get an understanding of the identified target group needs in the application.

Compile research material

The interview material was transcribed and compiled into an affinity diagram. The result of the diagram gave me insights of the target groups needs and features the target group would prefer in the application.

Research summary

Contact information: The owner wants access to the lender’s phone number or SMS contact.

Mileage: Feature that shows how far the car has been driven when it was lent out.

Time limit: A person should be allowed to lend the car in a limited time.

Lending to family and friends: The car owner prefers to lend the car to family and friends.

Other lenders: The owner may consider lending the car to strangers under special circumstances.

Required information: Only name and phone number for people they trust.

Lending history: Useful if the owner lends to many people or strangers.

GDPR compliance: The car owner should follow GDPR regulations when handling personal data.

Key functionality: The app should be as convenient as using a physical car key.

3. Idea generation: Sketches, Brainwriting 6-3-5

Sketches

To generate ideas of how to make the features of the application user-friendly, me and the project group created different sketches. The purpose was to generate a first vision of how the concept can be designed.

Brainwriting 6-3-5

After the first sketches were made, the project group conducted a brainstorming method called brainwriting 6-3-5. This was conducted to create multiple ideas of the concept to not get stuck in one idea.

Wireframes

Digital low- and mid-fidelity wireframes was created to develop different design solutions for the information architecture. This approach helped me avoid focusing on a final prototype before ensuring the design decisions and information architecture were user-friendly.

4. Prototyping: Develop prototypes

Develop prototypes

Fully functional mid-fidelity prototypes was developed. The purpose of doing them functional and mid-fidelity was because we were going to use them in evaluations, and wanted the test-persons to focus on the navigation and not the visual aspects.

5. Evaluations and Iterations: Usability tests, Iterate prototype

Usability tests

Evaluations in form of usability tests was conducted to ensure the interface was user-friendly. Different tasks was identified for the test-persons to complete during the evaluation and a short interview was conducted afterwards.

Iterate prototype

Based on the insights we received during the usability tests, the prototype was iterated. The purpose of the iteration was to make sure the lacks of the prototype was developed to a more user-friendly solution.

6. Final prototype: Develop final prototype

Develop final prototype

When I could ensure the interface was user-friendly, a functional high-fidelity prototype was developed. The goal was to make the prototype as realistic as possible, allowing Volvo to gain a clear understanding of the application and its features and navigation.

Result: Design Solutions

Share Car

Most users want to lend their car to family and friends. Therefore, the interface was designed to include a favorites list and a button linked to the phone’s contact book.

Location Service

Car owners want to track the cars location, those lending preferred not to be tracked. A feature that allows the lender to accept or decline the offer was implemented.

Lending

Users often lend their cars to family members. To avoid the need to always send new sharing requests, a feature that allows users to share the car indefinitely was added.

Sharing Options

Two options to share the car was implemented. A QR-code to imitate a physical handover and a SMS option to make the sharing more accessible. The lender doesn’t need an account.

Customization for Lender

Two separate interfaces were designed, one for car owners and one for lenders. Features like car-sharing are not included for lenders. Lenders dosen't need an account.

Digital Car Key

The digital key should work as a physical. Users can lock, unlock, and open the trunk in the application. Users should not lose any functions by using the digital key.

Menu

A side menu was implemented. Through this, the information architecture became logical, based on user evaluations. The menu also opens up possibilities for future development.

History & Privacy

The history features are, seeing who has used the car most, viewing where the car was last parked, adjust lendings and the option to cancel or extend a sharing arrangement.

Security

To ensure high security, Face-ID was implemented for critical actions like locking, unlocking and sharing. Face-ID can’t be turned off. If Face-ID cant be used, the phone's pin-code is used.

Insights

Communication with the project owner: I learned about the importance of having good a communication with the project owner.

Motivate all design choices: I realized that I always will have to be able to motivate all design choices based on research.

Information architecture: I learned about the importance of information architecture. It determines the navigation and therefore, the usability.

Design for users: I realized that it’s important to design for the users and not for anyone else.

Iteration & Evaluations: The value of iteration and evaluations. It can help to iterate prototypes and make them more user-friendly.
